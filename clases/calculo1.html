Calculus 1



Syllabus

  1. Introduction

    • History
    • The problems that founded calculus

  2. Real Numbers

    • Properties of real numbers and operations
    • Completeness property of the real line

  3. Functions and Sequences

    • Definition and examples of functions.
    • Elementary properties of functions
    • Definition of real number sequences
    • Cauchy sequences
    • Sum and product of functions and sequences
    • Composition of functions and inverses

  4. Limit

    • Formal definition of limit of a sequence
    • Convergence criteria
    • Limit of functions and examples
    • Limits of sums and products of functions
    • Limits that involve infinity

  5. Continuity

    • Definition of continuity
    • Basic properties
    • Intermediate value theorem

  6. Derivatives

    • Rate of change and speed
    • Tangents to curves
    • Definition and examples of derivatives
    • Relationship between continuity and differentiability
    • Derivative of sum, product and division of functions
    • Chain rule
    • Implicit derivatives
    • Higher order derivatives
    • Mean value theorem
    • Critical points
    • Finding maximum and minimum of a function
    • Taylor series
    • L'Hopital rule

Bibliography:

  • Courant, R., John, F., Introducción al Cálculo y al Análisis. México: Editorial Limusa, 1974..
  • Spivak, M., Cálculo Infinitesimal (2a ed). México: Reverté, 1998.
