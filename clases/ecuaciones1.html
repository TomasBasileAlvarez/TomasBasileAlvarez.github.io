Differential Equations 1



Syllabus

  1. Introduction

    • Basic Notions
    • Vector spaces over \( R^n \) and their associated differential equation
    • Phase space
    • Existence and uniqueness theorems

  2. Linear Differential Equations of First Order

    • Homogeneous equations
    • Non homogeneous equations, variation of parameters
    • Theorem of existence and uniqueness

  3. Non Linear Differential Equations of First Order

    • Separable equations. Integrating factos
    • Examples and applications
    • Picard's existence and uniqueness theorem
    • Integral equation. Picard's iterations
    • Gronwall's lemma

  4. Linear Differential Equations of Second Order

    • Homogeneous equations with constant coefficients
    • Properties of the set of solutions. Linear independence, Wronskian
    • General solution
    • Non homogeneous equations. Variation of parameters
    • Mechanical vibrations
    • Oscillations, resonance

  5. Linear Differential Equations of Second Order with Variable Coefficients

    • Solution by power series
    • Radius of convergence
    • Frobenious method
    • Hermite, Laguerre, Euler, Bessel, Legendre, Tchebucheff equations

  6. Laplace and Fourier Transform

    • Solution of equations using transformations

  7. Systems of linear differential equations

    • Reductionof an equation of order n to a system of n equations of first order
    • System of homogeneous differential equations
    • Linearly independent solutions
    • Wronskian
    • General solution and fundamental matrix. Exponential of a matrix
    • Non homogeneous system
    • Variation of parameters
    • Existence and uniqueness theorem
    • Examples and applications

  8. Qualitative theory

    • Stability and equilibrium of solutions of systems of linear differential equations
    • Classification of equilibrium points
    • Linearization of equilibrium points in a system of non linear equations
    • Poincare bendixon theorem

  9. Numerical Methdos

    • Euler method
    • Runge Kutta method

Bibliography:

  • Arnold, V.I., Ordinary Differential Equations (3ª ed.). Berlin: Springer-Verlag, 1992.
  • Blanchard, P., Devaney, R., Hall, G.,. Ecuaciones Diferenciales. México: International Thomson Editores, 1999.
  • Braun, M., Differential Equations and their Applications. New York: Springer-Verlag,1993
