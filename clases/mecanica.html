Classical Mechanics



Syllabus

  1. Introduction

    • The study of mechanics
    • Measurements and units

  2. Vectors

    • Vector algebra
    • Vectors as the language of mechanics

  3. Kinematics

    • Rectilinear motion. Velocity and acceleration
    • Vector representation of velocity and acceleration
    • Movement with constant acceleration
    • Tangential and normal components of acceleration
    • Circular motion
    • General curvilinear motion

  4. Dynamics

    • Newton's first law
    • Momentum
    • Newton's second and third law
    • Friction
    • Systems with variable mass
    • Angular moment and torque
    • Central forces

  5. Galilean Relativity

    • Relative velocity
    • Relative translation motion
    • Relative rotational motion

  6. Work and Energy

    • Work
    • Kinetic energy
    • Work for a constant force
    • Potential energy
    • Conservation of energy
    • Energy-work theorem
    • Movement under conservative forces
    • Non conservative forces

  7. System of particles

    • Total mass and total momentum
    • Center of mass
    • Angular momentum of a system of particles
    • Kinetic energy of a system of particles
    • Conservation of energy

  8. Rigid Body

    • Angular momentum of a rigid body
    • Calculation of moment of inertia
    • Equation of motion
    • Rotational kinetic energy

  9. Oscillations

    • Harmonic oscillator
    • Simple pendulum
    • Superposition of harmonic motions

  10. Gravity

    • Gravitational force
    • Newton's law of universal gravitation
    • Gravitational field of a sphere
    • Potential energry
    • General motion under gravitational forces
    • Kepler's laws
    • Equivalence principle
