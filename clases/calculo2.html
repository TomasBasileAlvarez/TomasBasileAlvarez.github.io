Calculus 2



Syllabus

  1. Definite Integral

    • Area under curves
    • Supreme and infimum
    • Superior and inferior sums
    • Definition of definite integral (Riemann sums)
    • Basic properties
    • Mean value theorem
    • Integrable and non integrable functions

  2. Fundamental Theorem of Calculus

    • Indefinite integral
    • Properties of indefinite integral
    • Proof of the fundamental theorems of calculus
    • Direct integration
    • Improper integrals

  3. Logarithm and Exponential

    • Definition of logarithm using the integral
    • Properties of logarithms
    • Exponential function
    • Properties of exponentials
    • Logarithmic derivative
    • Elliptical functions

  4. Integration Methodd

    • Change of variables
    • Integration by parts
    • Partial fractions
    • Trigonometric substitution
    • Numerical integration

  5. Applications

    • Taylor series and Cauchy residue
    • Calculating areas
    • Curve length
    • Volume and area of solids of revolution
    • Work, density and mass
    • Calculation of moments

  6. Series

    • Definition and examples of convergent and non convergent series
    • Convergence criteria
    • Alternating series
    • Leibniz creterium
    • Power series
    • Examples of Fourier Series

Bibliography:

  • Courant, R., John, F., Introducción al Cálculo y al Análisis. México: Editorial Limusa, 1974..
  • Spivak, M., Cálculo Infinitesimal (2a ed). México: Reverté, 1998.
  • Lang. S., Cálculo I. México: Fondo Educativo Interamericano, 1990.
