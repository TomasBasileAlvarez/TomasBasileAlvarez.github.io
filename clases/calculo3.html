Calculus 3



Syllabus

  1. Functions from \( R \) to \( R^N \)

    • Functions from \( R \) to \( R^N \) as curves in space. Limits and derivatives
    • Velocity and tangent vector
    • Rectifiable curves and arc length parametrization
    • Curvature and torsion
    • Examples of curves in the plane and space
    • Ferret and Serret system

  2. Normed Spaces

    • Vector spaces
    • Norm and inner product in \( R^N \)
    • p-norms

  3. Topology of \( R^N\)

    • Open and closed sets. Boundary of a set
    • Compact sets. Heine Borel theorem
    • Connected sets

  4. Functions from \( R^N\) to \( R^M \)

    • Cylindrical, polar and spherical coordinates
    • Functions from \( R^N\) to \( R^M \). Limits and continuity
    • Theorems of continuity in compact and connected sets
    • Bolzano Weierstrass theorem
    • Differentiability. Jacobian. Chain rule. Gradients
    • Implicit function theorem
    • Inverse function theorem
    • Laplacian, divergence and curl

  5. Maximum and Minimum

    • Critical poitns. Hessian matrix
    • Maximum and minimum with restrictions. Lagrange multipliers

Bibliography:

  • Courant, R., Differential and Integral Calculus, Volumen II. New York: J. Wiley, 1936
  • Courant, R., John, F., Introducción al Cálculo y al Análisis Matemático, Volumen II. México: Limusa, 1974.
  • Apostol, T.M., Calculus, Volumen I. México: Ed. Reverté, 2001.
