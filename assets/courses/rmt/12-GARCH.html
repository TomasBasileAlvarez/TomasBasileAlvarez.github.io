ARCH¶

Autoregressive Conditional Heteroskedasticity. Describes the variance $\sigma^2$ of a random variable with the following equation:

$$ \sigma_t^2 = \gamma V_L + \sum_{i=1}^Q \alpha_i r_{t-i}^2$$

$r$ is the logarithmic return of the asset whose variance is being modelled. $VL$ is the long term variance. $\gamma$ and $\alpha$ are weights such that $\gamma + \sum \alpha =1$.

GARCH¶

This adds a moving average to the equation.

$$ \sigma_t^2 = \gamma V_L + \sum_{i=1}^Q \alpha_i r_{t-i}^2 + \sum_{j=1}^P \beta_j \sigma_{t-j}^2 $$

So it has two parameters now, $p$ and $q$. We now have that $\gamma + \sum \alpha + \sum \beta =1$.
